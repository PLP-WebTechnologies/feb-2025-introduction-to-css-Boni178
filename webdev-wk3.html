Chakalaka Recipe

Chakalaka

Chakalaka is a spicy South African vegetable relish that is packed with flavor and often served as a side dish. It is believed to have originated in Johannesburg, created by mine workers who mixed canned beans, vegetables, and spices to add flavor to their meals. Over time, it became a staple in South African cuisine, enjoyed at barbecues (braais) and family gatherings.

Instructions

  • Heat the oil in a pan over medium heat.
  • Add the chopped onions and green bell pepper. Sauté until soft.
  • Stir in the garlic, grated carrots, curry powder, and paprika. Cook for 2 minutes.
  • Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
  • Mix in the baked beans and chopped chili (if using).
  • Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  • Let it simmer for 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  • Remove from heat and serve warm.

How is it served?

Chakalaka is traditionally served as a side dish alongside:
Pap (maize porridge) – A staple in South African homes.
Stews and Curries – Adds a spicy kick to meat or vegetable stews.
Braai (BBQ) – Complements grilled meats like chicken, beef, or boerewors (sausage).
Bread or Rice – A perfect dipping relish for fresh bread or a topping for rice.
